FAQs
How does an FSA help me save money?
An FSA lets you save money by paying for eligible expenses with tax-free money.
The money you contribute is deducted directly from your paycheck pre-tax. For example, if you’re in a 30% tax bracket, you’ll save $30 for every $100 you contribute to your FSA.
What are eligible FSA expenses?
For a Health Care FSA, eligible expenses include your deductible and coinsurance for medical, dental and vision expenses, as well as some additional over-the-counter and prescription health care items. For more information about what’s eligible, visit www.irs.gov.
For a Dependent Care FSA, eligible expenses include childcare costs for children up to the age of 13 or elder care.
What are eligible FSA expenses?
The IRS controls how much you can contribute to an FSA annually. For 2016, you can contribute up to:
  • $2,550 to a Health Care FSA
• $5,000 to a Dependent Care FSA
       
What if I don’t use all the money in my account by December 31, 2016?
The money you contribute to your FSA is use it or lose it – if you don’t spend all of the money in your account by the end of the year, you’ll lose it, so choose how much you contribute carefully. After the plan year ends December 31, 2016, you’ll have until February 28, 2017 to spend any remaining funds, and you’ll have until March 31, 2017 to claim those expenses for reimbursement.
