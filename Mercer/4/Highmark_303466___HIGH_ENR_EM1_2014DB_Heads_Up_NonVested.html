Dear HERRP Participant:

You participate in the Highmark Executive Retirement and Restoration Plan (commonly called the "HERRP"), a nonqualified cash balance pension plan that restores benefits that you would otherwise forfeit due to mandatory IRS limits.

HERRP benefits are subject to Social Security and Medicare taxes (commonly called "FICA" taxes) once you become vested in your nonqualified benefits. You will become vested in your nonqualified benefits once you reach age 55 and complete at least 3 years of service with the company. You will also become vested once you reach age 65 regardless of your service at that time.

Since you are not projected to satisfy the plan’s vesting rules as of December 31, 2014, you are neither required nor permitted to pay 2014 FICA taxes on the value of your nonqualified HERRP pension credits.

Additional information coming later this year

Later this month, some HERRP participants will receive personalized statements regarding their 2014 HERRP pension credits. These individuals will receive this special mailing because they satisfied the plan’s vesting provisions and must pay 2014 FICA taxes on their 2014 HERRP pension credits. Since you will not be vested as of December 31, 2014, this material will not apply to you.

However, before the end of the year, you will receive additional information regarding your HERRP account. This personalized information will summarize many HERRP plan provisions, including the need to complete a 409A distribution election (if this is the first time that you are enrolled in the plan) and a reminder to rebalance your HERRP account balance on myHighmark to maximize the self-invested feature of your nonqualified cash balance account.

 
OTHER NONQUALIFIED PLANS

This memo pertains only to the HERRP. If you also participate in additional employer-sponsored nonqualified plans, you may owe 2014 FICA taxes on some or all of these benefits. Further information regarding other nonqualified plans in which you may participate will be forthcoming shortly.
