HOW DO THE FLEXIBLE SPENDING ACCOUNTS (FSAs) WORK?

Health Care FSA [Limited Purpose FSA] Dependent Care FSA
Who’s eligible? Employees who are not enrolled in the HSA (available to CDHP members only) CDHP members who are enrolled in the HSA All employees are eligible
Contribution limits for 2016 From [$260] to [$2,550] From [$260] to [$2,550] From [$260] to [$5,000] ($2,500 if married filing separately)
Use this account for…
  • Deductibles, coinsurance, and copayments for medical, dental, and vision care
  • Prescription drugs
  • Eye exams, glasses, contact lenses and solution, and sunglasses when prescribed for a medical condition
  • Hearing exams and hearing aids
  • Lasik surgery
  • Adult orthodontia
  • And more — for a complete list, refer to IRS Publication 502, available at www.irs.gov.
  • Deductibles, coinsurance, and copayments for dental and vision care only (your HSA covers your out-of-pocket medical expenses)
  • Eye exams, glasses, contact lenses and solution, and sunglasses when prescribed for a medical condition
  • Hearing exams and hearing aids
  • Lasik surgery
  • Adult orthodontia
  • And more — for a complete list, refer to IRS Publication 502, available at www.irs.gov.
  • Licensed nursery school and child care centers
  • Private day care providers and nannies
  • Licensed care for disabled dependents
  • Care for an elderly parent whom you claim as a dependent on your tax return
  • Education expenses for a child not yet in the first grade, such as day care center expenses
  • Fees for special camps if the camp also serves as day care for your eligible dependent
  • And more — for a complete list, refer to IRS Publication 503, available at www.irs.gov.
Note: Your dependent care provider must have a federal tax identification or Social Security number in order for your expenses to be eligible for reimbursement.
