3 THINGS TO KNOW ABOUT FSAs

  • Enroll if you want to participate. Even if you currently participate in an FSA, you must re-enroll if you want to participate in 2016. FSA enrollment does not roll over from year to year.
  • Contribute only as much as you’ll use. [If no carryover: Before deciding how much you want to contribute, you should carefully estimate your 2016 expenses, because you will lose any amount remaining in your FSAs at the end of the calendar year.] [If carryover: Before deciding how much you want to contribute, you should carefully estimate your 2016 expenses. Up to $500 of unused funds remaining in your account at the end of 2016 may be carried over for use in 2017, but amounts exceeding $500 will be forfeited.]
  • Save your receipts. It’s important to keep your receipts whenever you swipe your Health Care FSA debit card. You may be asked to provide them as documentation to verify a claim.
