How much could you save with a Consumer Choice Plan?

The following stories give you an idea of how the cost of the Consumer Choice Plan options might compare to the PPO plan options. These examples are for illustrative purposes only; your actual expenses may vary.

Looking for low-cost healthcare

Brad is in his late 20s and is generally healthy. On average, he only sees his doctor a couple of times a year – mostly for preventive care. Because the employee-only premium for the Consumer Choice Core Plan is just $75.00 per month and his preventive care is free (just like in the PPO plans), Brad would save the most money by enrolling in this plan. As an added bonus, Hillenbrand, Inc. will contribute $750.00 to Brad’s HSA in 2013 – that’s like “free money” that he wouldn’t receive if he chose a PPO plan. And, since he doesn’t need to spend that contribution in 2013, he can roll it over year over year and use it to pay for future health expenses.

Family coverage with two children

Kim provides health coverage for her husband and two kids through Hillenbrand. To decide which coverage she should choose for 2013, she thinks back to her family's health care needs over the past year. Her daughter caught strep throat in the spring, and then had an ear infection shortly thereafter and needed a prescription treatment. Later in the year, Kim's son had an allergic reaction and visited the allergist for an evaluation and treatment. And in the fall, her husband had an MRI scan to help determine the cause of his back pain, and his doctor recommended that he begin physical therapy.

Given that her family has a wide variety of health care needs in a typical year, Kim assumes that she would pay a substantial amount more out of pocket by participating in a Consumer Choice Plan as opposed to a PPO plan. However, with its lower premiums and Hillenbrand's contribution to the HSA, she sees that the Consumer Choice Core Plan is ultimately the most cost-effective choice.

Saving for future health care expenses 

For the past two years, Robert and his wife and son have been covered under Hillenbrand, Inc.’s Consumer Choice plan. He knows he now has two Consumer Choice Plans to choose from for 2013 – as well as two PPO options – and wants to determine which plan he should enroll in. To help estimate their health care expenses for 2013, he thinks about their health care needs last year. He remembers that he caught the flu in the spring, and then his son had a sinus infection that was treated with a prescription. His wife visited a dermatologist for psoriasis a few months later and began using a prescription treatment. Then in the fall, his wife began having severe knee pain. The MRI scan indicated that she needed surgery for which she spent three days in the hospital and began physical therapy a few weeks later.

Despite many health care needs and even a costly procedure like his wife’s inpatient knee surgery, Robert can see that, at $5,524, the Consumer Choice Buy-Up Plan costs him less than the other plan options. Plus, he likes the idea of continuing to accumulate money in his HSA to help cover their healthcare costs in retirement. In fact, thanks to his – and Hillenbrand’s – contributions to his HSA over the past two years, he has accumulated over $5,000 so far. He plans to dip into this money as needed each year to help cover costs.
