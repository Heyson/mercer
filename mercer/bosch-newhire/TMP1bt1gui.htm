New for 2014

We encourage you to carefully review what’s changing to help you select the most appropriate coverage for 2014.

What’s new beginning January 1, 2014

Higher Health Savings Account (HSA) contribution limits: In the 2014 calendar year, the maximum HSA contribution from you and Bosch combined is limited to $3,300 per person (up from $3,250 in 2013) and $6,550 per family (up from $6,450 in 2013). If you’re age 55 or older, you may contribute an additional $1,000 annually.

Health care reform changes: While parts of the Affordable Care Act have been implemented each year since it was passed in 2010, there are some important changes taking effect in 2014:

  • Individual mandate – Beginning in 2014, all Americans (with a few exceptions) are required to have basic health insurance coverage or pay a penalty when filing their federal income tax returns. Enrolling in a health plan offered through Bosch will meet this requirement.
  • Health insurance marketplace – Qualified individuals may choose to purchase health insurance through the public health insurance marketplace. The primary intent of the marketplace is to help people who do not have access to affordable insurance. At Bosch, you are offered comprehensive, affordable insurance that provides better coverage at a lower cost, so selecting one of the Company’s medical plans is probably a better option for you than buying through the marketplace.
  • Tax credits – You may have heard about tax credits and other types of financial assistance the federal government will be providing to help individuals pay for insurance purchased through the marketplace. This assistance is only available to those who meet very specific criteria, including income requirements. For more information, visit www.healthcare.gov.
  • No annual dollar limits – Health plans are now prohibited from imposing annual dollar limits on essential benefits, like hospital stays. These limits had already been removed from Bosch’s medical plans, so there will be no new impact to you for 2014.
  • Pre-existing conditions – Insurance companies are now prohibited from refusing to provide coverage or renew policies because of an individual's pre-existing medical conditions. Bosch’s medical plans did not have this exclusion before, so there will be no new impact to you for 2014.
What’s new beginning April 1, 2014

The following benefit changes apply to Power Tools associates only.
Starting April 1, 2014, you will have the same health benefit choices and plans as the rest of the Bosch non-union associates. As a result of this new approach, there will be several benefit changes that will affect you:

  • Choice of dental plan options: Your dental coverage options will be expanded. You will have a choice of two dental plans, both administered by MetLife: the 80% Preventive Plan (without orthodontia) or the 100% Preventive Plan (with orthodontia). Although both plans allow you to visit any dentist you choose, you will save more when you see providers in the MetLife network. Learn more about your dental plans.
  • Vision plan coverage: Enrollment in the VSP will no longer be automatic when you enroll in the medical plan. If you want vision coverage in the 2014 plan year, you will need to actively elect it during the Annual Enrollment period. You will also be responsible for 100% of the cost of coverage. Learn more about the vision plan.
  • Life and AD&D insurance: Currently, basic associate life insurance and accidental death & dismemberment (AD&D) insurance — which are automatically provided by Bosch at no cost to you — are offered as a combined benefit. Effective April 1, 2014, these benefits will be provided separately (but still automatically and at no cost to you). Optional associate life and AD&D insurance will also be provided as separate benefits, and you will have the opportunity to purchase additional associate life and/or AD&D insurance coverage up to a maximum of eight times your annual base salary. Your current beneficiary information on file for these benefits will remain in effect. Learn more about your insurance benefits.
  • Long-term disability coverage: Basic long-term disability (LTD) insurance coverage, which is automatically provided by Bosch at no cost to you, will replace 50% of your monthly base pay. In addition to basic LTD, you may purchase additional LTD insurance for a total income replacement benefit of 65% of your monthly base pay. During this upcoming Annual Enrollment period only, you may enroll in this additional buy-up coverage without providing Evidence of Insurability (EOI). Learn more about your insurance benefits.

The following benefit changes apply to all associates and are a result of the Affordable Care Act (ACA).

  • Clinical trials: Insurers will be prohibited from dropping or limiting coverage because an individual chooses to participate in a clinical trial. This rule applies to all clinical trials that treat cancer or other life-threatening diseases.
  • PPO copays (for Expatriates enrolled in the PPO Basic or PPO Plus medical plan options): Flat dollar copays you pay for healthcare will count toward your annual out-of-pocket maximum and annual deductible. Please note that prescription drug copays will not count toward your annual out-of-pocket maximum or annual deductible.
