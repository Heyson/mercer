New for 2015

We encourage you to carefully review what’s changing to help you select the most appropriate coverage for 2015.

What’s new beginning January 1, 2015

Higher Health Savings Account (HSA) contribution limits: In the 2015 calendar year, the maximum HSA contribution from you and Bosch combined is limited to $3,350 per person (up from $3,300 in 2014) and $6,650 per family (up from $6,550 in 2014). If you’re age 55 or older, you may contribute an additional $1,000 annually.

What’s new beginning April 1, 2015

Increased medical plan annual deductibles: The Consumer Driven Health Plan (CDHP) 80 annual deductible will be $1,900 individual/$3,800 family (up from $1,850 individual/$3,650 family) and the CDHP 90 annual deductible will be $1,300 individual/$2,600 family (up from $1,250 individual/$2,500 family).

Higher FSA contribution limit: The maximum you can contribute to a Health Care FSA (any Bosch associate who does not have a Health Savings Account (HSA) is eligible) or a Limited Purpose FSA (any Bosch associate enrolled in a CDHP who also has an HSA is eligible) will increase from $2,500 to $2,550. The minimum you can contribute remains $120.

Changes to dependent eligibility: Your children will be eligible for medical, dental, vision, life insurance, and accidental death & dismemberment (AD&D) insurance coverage until the end of the month in which they turn age 26.

MORE INFORMATION

You have access to a Summary of Benefits and Coverage (SBC) for each of your medical plan options. These documents provide detailed information about coverage and costs to help you compare plans and make informed decisions. Access SBCs for your plan options by visiting the Bosch Benefits Web site at www.ibenefitcenter.com, visiting the “Plan Information” page of the “Resource Center,” and clicking “Health.” You can also call the Bosch Benefits Center at 800-207-9012 to request printed copies.
