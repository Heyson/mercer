Medical plans

As a Bosch associate, you have a choice of two Consumer Driven Health Plan (CDHP) options: CDHP 80 and CDHP 90. Both are administered through BlueCross BlueShield of Illinois (BCBSIL).

WHAT IS A CDHP?

Consumer Driven Health Plans, or CDHPs, encourage you to lead a healthy lifestyle and to buy smart when you need healthcare. In turn, they offer you a tax-free Health Savings Account (HSA), lower premiums than traditional types of health insurance plans, and free in-network preventive care.

1. Tax-advantaged savings account

When you enroll in one of Bosch’s CDHPs, you can open a Health Savings Account (HSA) — it’s one of the best perks of this type of plan. Each year, to help you cover your out-of-pocket health costs, Bosch will contribute to your HSA on your behalf:

  • $500 for individual coverage and $1,000 for family coverage (family coverage is any other coverage level other than employee-only) if base pay is $50,000 or more. Note that amounts may be prorated based on your date of hire.
  • $595 for individual coverage and $1,190 for family coverage (family coverage is any other coverage level other than employee-only) if base pay is less than $50,000. Note that amounts may be prorated based on your date of hire.

All contributions to and withdrawals from your account are tax-free, as long as you use the money to pay for eligible health-related expenses. Additionally, all the money in the account is yours and will never be forfeited. You can roll it over at the end of each year and take it with you if you leave the Company. You can even use it for health-related expenses during retirement.

2. Low-premium, high-deductible coverage

With the CDHPs, your per-paycheck premium costs are much lower compared to traditional HMO and PPO plan options, so you get to keep more of your paycheck each month. And, while the annual deductible is higher, you can use the tax-free money in your HSA to help cover your out-of-pocket costs.

With this type of plan, you follow a simple three-step process to pay for your nonpreventive medical expenses and prescription drugs:

  • You pay 100% of your initial medical costs until you meet your annual deductible.
  • After you meet your annual deductible, the plan pays a certain percentage of any further costs until you reach your annual out-of-pocket maximum (this is known as “coinsurance”).
  • Once you meet your out-of-pocket maximum, the plan pays 100% of your eligible expenses for the remainder of the year.

3. Free in-network preventive care

Preventive care is fully covered under the CDHPs, meaning you pay nothing toward your deductible and no copays as long as you receive this care from in-network providers. Common preventive care services are listed below. When you receive these services, be sure that your doctor codes the eligible procedure as routine or preventive to ensure that you receive the full Bosch benefit.

  • Female contraceptives (excluding brand name prescriptions with a generic equivalent).
  • Immunizations.
  • Routine blood work (e.g., glucose, cholesterol, iron).
  • Routine colorectal cancer screening.
  • Routine diagnostic tests (e.g., EKG, bone density tests).
  • Routine digital rectal exams.
  • Routine gynecological exams.
  • Routine mammograms.
  • Routine pap smear tests.
  • Routine prostate tests.
  • Routine urinalysis.
  • Well-baby care.
  • Wellness exams.
  • Certain preventive care prescription drugs.

For more information about this coverage, visit the CDHP Resource Center on the Bosch Benefits Web site.

COMPARE YOUR OPTIONS
The CDHP 80 or CDHP 90: Which is best for you?
Consider the amount of coinsurance you want Bosch to pay on your behalf — 80% or 90% — once you meet your annual deductible. While the CDHP 80 option has a higher deductible, you’ll also pay a lower per-paycheck premium. So, when making your decision, consider whether you prefer lower per-paycheck premiums in exchange for higher deductibles, or the opposite.
