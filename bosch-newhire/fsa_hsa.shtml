Health Savings Account/Flexible Spending Accounts

The Health Savings Account (HSA), Health Care Flexible Spending Account (FSA), and Dependent Day Care FSA can stretch your benefit dollars further by allowing you to pay for eligible expenses with tax-free dollars. Learn more about the advantages of these plans below.

HSA

When you enroll in Bosch’s CDHP 80 or CDHP 90 plan for 2016, you will also need to choose how much you wish to contribute, if anything, to an HSA. The HSA is a tax-sheltered savings account that can save you a significant amount on healthcare expenses, both now and in retirement. That’s because it allows you to pay for eligible medical expenses with tax-free dollars — and, as an added benefit, Bosch will contribute money into your account each year.

Click here to access detailed instructions for opening your HSA.

How it works

  • If you enroll in one of the Bosch CDHP options and open an HSA through the plan’s benefit provider, BenefitWallet, you will automatically receive a Bosch contribution to your account on a quarterly basis, even if you don’t contribute yourself. For 2016, Bosch’s total annual contribution to your HSA is:
    • $500 for individual coverage and $1,000 for family coverage (family coverage is any other coverage level other than employee-only) if base pay is $50,000 or more. Note that amounts may be prorated based on your date of hire.
    • $595 for individual coverage and $1,190 for family coverage (family coverage is any other coverage level other than employee-only) if base pay is less than $50,000. Note that amounts may be prorated based on your date of hire.
  • In addition to Bosch’s contributions on your behalf, you can elect to make additional pre- or after-tax contributions during the year, up to the calendar year maximum. In the 2016 calendar year, the maximum HSA contribution from you and Bosch combined is limited to $3,350 per person and $6,750 per family. If you’re age 55 or older, you may contribute an additional $1,000 annually.
  • Your BenefitWallet HSA will earn interest annually, adding to your account balance. Any interest or earnings on your money builds tax-free.*
  • You may withdraw the money tax free to pay for health-related expenses. Spend it by using a convenient HSA debit card or by writing checks from your HSA. You can also withdraw money by paying directly from your HSA online using Direct Pay HSA.*
  • The money in your HSA is yours to keep and can be rolled over from year to year (unlike the money in an FSA). You can take it with you if you leave the Company, and you can even use it for health-related expenses during retirement.

*Money in an HSA grows tax-free and can be withdrawn tax-free as long as it is used to pay for qualified health-related expenses (a list of eligible expenses can be found in IRS Publications 502 and 969, available at www.irs.gov). If money is used for ineligible expenses, you will pay ordinary income tax on the amount withdrawn. If you withdraw the money before age 65, you will also pay a 20% penalty tax.

Health Care FSAs

Bosch gives you the opportunity to save on taxes by depositing money from each paycheck into a Health Care FSA before taxes are taken out. You can set aside any amount between $120 and $2,550 each year and use it to pay for eligible health, dental, and vision care expenses (with a Traditional Health Care FSA), or for eligible dental and vision expenses only (with a Limited Purpose Health Care FSA). For instance, under either account, you could maximize your savings potential by using in-network vision and dental providers, then using the funds in your FSA to help offset costs not covered by the plan.

Traditional or Limited Purpose Health Care FSA? Your participation in an HSA will determine which type of Health Care FSA you can enroll in: Traditional or Limited Purpose. Because of the many tax advantages an HSA provides, if you have an HSA, the government only allows you to participate in a Limited Purpose Health Care FSA at the same time. If you don’t have an HSA but are enrolled in a CDHP, you may still participate in a Traditional Health Care FSA.

How they work
When you incur an eligible expense, you pay for it upfront and are reimbursed with pre-tax dollars from your account by PayFlex, the plan’s administrator. If you are enrolled in a BCBSIL plan and are not currently participating in an HSA, you will automatically be set up to participate in the crossover claims process with BCBSIL. Through this automatic claim submission process, all of your eligible medical out-of-pocket expenses will be automatically submitted to PayFlex by BCBSIL. PayFlex will then process the claim and issue a reimbursement to you directly via either direct deposit, or if chosen, paper check. If you prefer not to participate in the automatic claim submission process with BCBSIL, and you would rather submit claim forms yourself, you may contact the Bosch Benefits Center and request to opt out of the automatic claim submission process. If you are not enrolled in a BCBSIL plan or you incur eligible non-medical expenses, you must submit your receipts to PayFlex for reimbursement.

Be sure to save all of your receipts in case you need to submit them to PayFlex for validation purposes. Because you contribute to an FSA with pre-tax dollars, you lower your taxable income and, therefore, the amount of current tax you owe. Plus, you won’t ever pay those taxes, even when you’re reimbursed. However, unlike an HSA, it’s critical that you carefully estimate how much money you’ll need to cover your healthcare expenses for the year since you’ll forfeit any amount that remains in your FSA at year-end.

Dependent Day Care FSA

Bosch gives you the opportunity to save on taxes by depositing money from each paycheck into a Dependent Day Care FSA before taxes are taken out. You can set aside between $120 and $5,000 ($2,500 if you are married and file your taxes separately) each year and use it to pay for qualified child care expenses so you and your spouse/domestic partner can work or attend school full time. Expenses are qualified if used for a child under 13 or for the care of a physically or mentally disabled parent, child, or other relative of any age whom you claim as a dependent for federal tax purposes. Note that a dependent's healthcare expenses do not count as qualified dependent care expenses.

How it works
When you incur an eligible expense, you pay for it upfront and are reimbursed with pre-tax dollars from your account by PayFlex, the plan’s administrator. Because you contribute to an FSA with pre-tax dollars, you lower your taxable income and, therefore, the amount of current tax you owe. Plus, you won’t ever pay those taxes, even when you’re reimbursed. However, unlike an HSA, it’s critical that you carefully estimate how much money you’ll need to cover your day care expenses for the year since you’ll forfeit any amount that remains in your FSA at year-end.

Regardless of whether or not you have an HSA, you may participate in a Dependent Day Care FSA at the same time.
